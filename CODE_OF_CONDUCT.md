# Code of Conduct

This project follows the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).
This page summarises what that means in practice; the full text is
authoritative.

## In short

- Assume good faith. Most disagreements are about technical
  trade-offs, not character.
- Critique code, not people.
- Keep examples and discussion technical. Politics, religion, and
  identity-based jokes do not belong in issues, PRs, or chat.
- If someone asks you to stop a behaviour, stop and re-engage on
  the substance.

## Scope

These standards apply in every tinylink space: GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, and any in-person event under the tinylink
name.

## Reporting

If something needs an outside eye, email **paladei702@gmail.com**.
Reports are kept confidential. The maintainer will acknowledge
within 48 hours and respond with next steps within seven days.

## Consequences

In escalation order:
1. Private clarification with everyone involved.
2. Public correction in the thread where the incident occurred.
3. Temporary cool-off — typically 30 days from project spaces.
4. Permanent removal from the project for repeated or severe violations.

The maintainer makes the final call, with the same standard applied
to maintainers and contributors alike.
